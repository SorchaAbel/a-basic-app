Drama


Drama involves a lot of acting. At Fiona Keane's School of Speech and Drama we encourage all students to perform in plays. Drama can be a great tool for building character and self esteem. Children have an opportunity to dress up and perform in front of an audience, which is extremely fun and allows kids to be kids which we feel is important in todays fast paced, high tech society.

Acting is involved in all courses at this school whether it be role playing, improvisation, mime, poetry reading or miming to music. It is a great way for building confidence in public situations, learning about the theatre, team bonding and above all having fun. Fiona believes that a child's imagination is very important. Drama is a fantastic way for a child to explore their imagination and share the experience with peers of similar ages.


Drama consists of the following;


  • Role playing
  • Costume wearing
  • Acting in front of audiences
  • Watching other performances
  • Creating plays
