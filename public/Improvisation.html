Improvisation

Improvisation is an opportunity for children and young adults to explore their own creative potential in a lively and stimulating environment. It provides an outlet for self expression and helps the development of imagination and artistic awareness (particularly through role-play), mental awareness, fluency of speech and self discipline. It gives children the opportunity to learn how to co-operate with others, but most importantly, it provides enjoyment!

The Fiona Keane School of Speech and Drama ensures each subject in all classes are met with enthusiasm and dedication for pupils and teachers alike. While children gain a vast amount of knowledge and self development from this subject, fun remains the primary focus at all times.

All classes included in the curriculum have been developed with two goals in mind

  1. Enjoyment
  2. Fun!

Improvisation is included in all courses for children from Grade one (ages range from 4 plus) to Diploma level for adults.
