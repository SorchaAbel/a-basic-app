Frequently Asked Questions

Q. How many pupils in a class?

A. Class sizes are kept small to ensure individual attention. Numbers in each class are kept to approximately 12 to ensure each child gets the attention they deserve.

Q. My child is very shy?

A. Each child is treated as an individual. The point of the classes is to nurture each child and to develop their self confidence. This is done through plenty of fun and games and confidence building exercises. Each child is encouraged to work at their own pace.

Q. Does everyone do the Grade Exams?

A. Everyone is encouraged to take the grade exams at the end of the year however no body is forced into anything they are not entirely comfortable with, and so, the exams are of course optional.

Q. Explain the exams?

A. The exams are held in association with The Irish Board of Speech and Drama. The Speech, Drama and Communications exams encourage children, young people and adults to be better communicators, to increase their confidence, to develop their skills and talent and to foster their own creativity. The examiners are professional, encouraging and friendly, and generate a relaxed atmosphere so that candidates can perform to their best ability.

Q. What do you cover in class?

A. In class we cover a wide range of subjects with particular attention paid to improvisation, mime, movement to music, poetry, speech and acting.

back to top

Q. Do you do private tuition?

A. Yes. Private tuition is available by appointment.

back to top

Q. What ages do you teach?

A. The classes are suitable for pupils aged 4-14 years. The classes are streamlined to ensure children are in classes with other children of a similar age.

back to top

Q. Do you teach adults?

A. Yes. Adult tuition is available by appointment. The content of these classes would depend on the individuals requirements. Usual topics such as poetry, acting, improvisation, mime, movement to music and speech are covered as is public speaking and exam preparation (optional).
